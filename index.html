Digital Footprint

DEFINE

Every time we connect to the internet we always leave a trace. That is our real footprint. This can reveal where you have been, how long you have been there, and what you have been doing there.

TIPS

1.If you want to post comments online, you don’t have to use your own name. Invent a nickname to use instead. You can also use a picture instead of a real photo. 2.Review and adjust the privacy settings on your social media and online accounts to control who can see your information. 3.Don’t tell anyone your passwords and don’t write them down in an obvious place. Make them more complex by using a combination of letters, numbers and punctuation marks. 4.Protect your identity online. Be careful about who you share personal information with and always think before sharing details like your email, home address, school or phone number with someone. 5. Don`t upload your photos to social networks, this is dangerous because it will take away our privacy and we will be exposed, we will give information about where we are or our body to other people.

Drawbacks

1.Posting inappropriate images or comments may affect your personal or professional image.
2.Personal data can be stolen and use to fraud or identity theft.
3.Browsing data can be use to create profiles and send personalized advertising, that is invasive and manipulative.
4.The constant connection and the need to be online can generate dependency and affect mental health.
5.Posting incorrect information can lead to legal problems.
6.When information is online, it is difficult or impossible to delete it completely.

Responsibilities as a student

1. As a student, you're responsible for protecting yourself, your information, and your devices.
2. Respect your privacy and that of other students.
3.Don't share personal information or post things that could harm your or that of other students.
4. Take responsibility for your device's activity
5. Protect and clean your computer.
6. Use strong and unique passwords.
7. Protect your online reputation.
8. Understand the consequences for violating the rules.
